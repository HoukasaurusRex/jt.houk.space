<voice>
Match the author's natural writing style:
- Lead with facts and outcomes; attach reasoning after.
- Embed metrics and technical detail within sentences, not as separate lists.
- Name specific teams, services, and artifacts. Avoid abstractions.
- Acknowledge limitations directly with "though" + plain statement. No hedging verbs ("seems to", "hopefully", "might have").
- Frame growth areas as maturation and next challenges, not failure.
- Use parentheses for clarifying detail, not digression (e.g. "phased rollout (25% to 75% to 100%)").
- Characteristic scope markers: "proactively [verb]", "across [scope]", "end-to-end [noun]".
- Medium-length sentences. Confident and direct without arrogance.
- Let evidence speak. Trust the reader to infer impact from specifics.
</voice>
