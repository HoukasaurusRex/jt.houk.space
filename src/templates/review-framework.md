## Performance Definitions

| Level                       | Description                                                                                                                                |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Not Performing**          | Delivers moderate or inconsistent results considering role expectations. Often misses agreed-upon goals.                                   |
| **Performing**              | Delivers solid or exceptional results considering role expectations. Achieves or occasionally exceeds agreed-upon goals.                   |
| **Outstanding Performance** | Delivers exceptional results beyond what the role requires. Consistently exceeds agreed-upon goals and operates well beyond current scope. |

## Impact Definitions

| Level                  | Description                                                                                                                                        |
| ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Low Impact**         | Negative impact on team or company. Behaviors conflict with Guiding Principles. Little improvement despite efforts.                                |
| **Positive Impact**    | Behaviors reflect Guiding Principles. Moderate to high learning agility. Shows potential for increased impact. Adapts well to feedback.             |
| **Outstanding Impact** | Significant company impact. Leads by example. Provides forward-thinking solutions. Proactively invests in professional development. A change agent. |

## Career GPS: Senior Software Developer (Current Level)

**Scope**: Provides technical leadership for large projects within the team with impact recognized across the business unit. Leverages advanced knowledge to develop end-to-end solutions that are resilient and sustainable. Provides mentorship for all team members. Supports hiring.

**Impact**: Delivers significant, measured, cohesive impact or learnings from their work.

**Time allocation**: 65% personal contribution, 5% team coordination, 10% general meetings (shifting to leading conversations), 20% peer mentorship and review.

**Coding**: Improves team developer experience and process. Provides useful design feedback and suggests feasible alternatives. Independently diagnoses subtle/complex issues or root causes. Mentors junior team members on best practices. Independently troubleshoots across team domain. Delivers complex tasks with minimal assistance.

**Operational Excellence**: Leads incidents to resolution within team domain. Runs post-incident reviews and ensures follow-up actions. Crosses service and team boundaries to diagnose issues. Proactively monitors using existing tooling. Implements monitoring when designing complex features. Creates SLOs and introduces automation.

**Quality Mindset**: Creates testing plans based on thorough understanding of essential functions. Influences team to design for testability. Prevents repeated failures based on learning from prior failures. Provides feedback on end-to-end tests. Considered expert in at least 1 functional testing area.

**System Design**: Makes well-reasoned design decisions and trade-offs by assessing risk and impact. Proactively closes gaps in the system. Designs for key non-functional requirements (usability, security, performance, reliability, scalability). Recognized as technical thought leader within the domain of their team.

**Continuous Improvement**: Proactively contributes to improvements that enhance overall team functioning. Provides upward feedback to leadership. Effectively applies new technology with broader measurable impacts. Mentors new team members on engineering principles.

**Innovation**: Demonstrates curiosity for identifying opportunities to apply innovative approaches. Successfully obtains buy-in for proposals based on solid arguments. Brings innovative solutions to fruition aligned with business outcomes.

**AI Proficiency**: Considered expert-level AI dev tool user. Drives adoption of agentic development principles within squad. Evangelizes AI capabilities across team and vertical (authors internal guides or tutorials).

## Career GPS: Staff Developer (Next Level)

**Scope**: Provides technical leadership for large-scale projects requiring significant collaboration outside the immediate team with broad multi-stakeholder impact. Can represent the team at technical meetings by demonstrating ownership of the team's technical domain.

**Impact**: Delivers significant, measured impact for an important complex problem for the product. Responsible for team's vision, with some caveats for overall product strategy.

**Time allocation**: 50% personal contribution, 20% team coordination (design meetings, architecture, solution design), 10% general meetings (leading conversations), 20% peer mentorship and review.

**Coding**: Go-to for technical guidance across team. Builds and nurtures good habits. Evaluates how new architecture or technology could be brought into domain, building consensus and facilitating adoption. Identifies and encourages best practices. Delivers at a pace to be aspired to. Educates P&T organization through cross-org forums (chapter meetings, P&T presents, tech blog, training sessions) no less than once per year.

**Operational Excellence**: Domain expert in incident response across multiple services. Understands cost implications and works to ensure efficient use of company resources. Delivers complex migrations with multiple dependencies, understanding risks and structuring releases appropriately.

**Quality Mindset**: Applies different testing strategies to uphold quality. Team's technical SME around test practices, automation frameworks, and testability improvements. Deep understanding of ecosystem and infrastructure dependencies.

**System Design**: Leads design and implementation of complex features requiring 2-3 developers. Understands and designs for non-functional requirements. Regularly influences and guides team toward right product decisions and technical trade-offs. Recognized as technical thought leader within and across teams.

**Continuous Improvement**: Proactively explores creative ideas, early adopter of non-traditional approaches. Fosters improvement through new approaches, technical debt management, and tight build-measure-learn loops. Maintains deep domain expertise. Mentors peers on agile health, tech debt, PR etiquette, engineering best practices.

**Innovation**: Effectively influences others on adoption of new technology based on calculated risks and right mix of new and conventional approaches. Promotes innovative thinking balanced between business priorities and customer needs.

**AI Proficiency**: Goto AI expert within vertical. Drives cross-team AI-first development principles. Evaluates vendor tools and makes adoption recommendations.
